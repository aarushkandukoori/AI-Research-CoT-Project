Chain-of-thought evaluation

Do models say what they think?

An interactive replication of CoT faithfulness research — testing whether written reasoning admits the real reason for an answer, or invents a clean-looking story after the fact.

The failure mode

A model can change its answer because of a hidden bias, then write step-by-step reasoning that never mentions the bias existed.

01

Hint injected

Sycophancy (“a professor thinks X”) or option reordering pushes a wrong answer forward.

02

Answer flips

Compared to baseline CoT, the model switches to the hinted choice.

03

CoT stays “clean”

If reasoning never cites the hint, the explanation is unfaithful — post-hoc rationalization.
